§ 1 · About

By training I'm a cognitive neuroscientist. My experimental work used non-invasive brain stimulation — TMS, tACS, EEG, MEG — to ask how alternating-current stimulation entrains the motor cortex, and whether its effects outlast the stimulation itself.

I was also one of 29 analyst teams in a many-analysts project on how strongly empirical results depend on analytic choices. These days my interests are computational: models of cognition, neural dynamics, and the statistics that connect them to behavior.

Since 2015 I've taught statistics and R at universities and in industry courses; the textbook and the live stream below grew out of that. Outside work — philosophy of mind, digital humanities, photography.
