§ 1 · About

I'm a cognitive neuroscientist by training — brain stimulation and neuroimaging: TMS, tACS, EEG, MEG — with a decade of statistics, programming, and teaching layered on top of the experimental foundation.

My research background is in non-invasive brain stimulation: I studied how transcranial alternating current stimulation entrains the motor cortex, and when its effects outlast the stimulation itself. I was also one of the analyst teams in a large many-analysts project on how strongly empirical results depend on analytic choices. These days I think about cognition computationally — neural dynamics, decision-making, and the statistical machinery that connects models to behavioral data.

I teach statistics and R the way I wish they were taught to me: university courses, industry intensives, a free textbook, and a weekly live-coding stream. Beyond work — philosophy of mind, digital humanities, photography, and the occasional typeface.
